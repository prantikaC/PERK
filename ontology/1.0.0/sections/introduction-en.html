Introduction back to ToC

Digital sources of personal information — emails, chats, browser history — are a rich but underused basis for personal agents and for personal knowledge graphs (PKGs) that organise information about their owner. Emails in particular are one of the most pervasive forms of personal communication and a detailed record of a researcher's professional life, yet one of the least tapped knowledge resources.

PERKOnto is the ontology underpinning PERK (Personal Email Research Knowledge-Graph), a personal research knowledge graph derived from a researcher's emails. It models the entities and relations that matter to a researcher's academic activity — collaborators, publication details and lifecycle, scientific terminology (tasks, methods, datasets, metrics), and meetings — while deliberately omitting bulk text and capturing only precise, crucial information. The ontology was developed with the Ontology Development 101 methodology and is driven by ten competency questions, such as a researcher's collaborators and the tasks they share, a paper's full submission and revision history, and in which thread a dataset or task was first discussed.

Unlike output-oriented scholarly vocabularies (e.g. ORKG, SWRC) and general communication ontologies, PERKOnto is process-oriented and provenance-aware. It reuses and specialises FOAF, BIBO, Schema.org, and PROV-O via rdfs:subClassOf/rdfs:subPropertyOf; treats email as a first-class provenance substrate, with every triple recording the source emails from which it was derived; and models the publication lifecycle as a state machine (SubmissionID —movesTo→ PaperStatus). PERKOnto is deployed as a Neo4j labelled property graph and published as OWL/Turtle for Linked Data interoperability. Although instantiated here for a computer-science researcher working in Natural Language Processing, it is readily adaptable to other domains by replacing or extending the domain-specific Task, Method, Dataset, and Metric instances. It underpins the PERK pipeline for LLM-based, ontology-guided knowledge-graph construction and question answering over the synthetic PATRA corpus.
