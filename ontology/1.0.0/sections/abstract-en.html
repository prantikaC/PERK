Abstract

PERKOnto (Personal Email Research Knowledge Ontology) is an OWL ontology for modelling the professional knowledge of an academic researcher as expressed in personal email. Developed following the Ontology Development 101 methodology and driven by ten competency questions, it comprises 14 mutually disjoint OWL classes and 14 object properties organised into four clusters: Communication Infrastructure (Email, MailThread), Researchers and Venues (Person, Conference, Journal), Publication Lifecycle (Paper, PaperBib, SubmissionID, PaperStatus), and Research Content (Dataset, Method, Task, Metric, Meeting). A defining feature is provenance: every object property carries a source property recording the emails from which the assertion was derived (aligned to prov:wasDerivedFrom), supporting end-to-end traceability and corroboration during entity resolution. PERKOnto provides the schema for PERK, a Personal Email Research Knowledge graph, and was used to construct and query a knowledge graph over PATRA — a synthetic corpus of academic emails — evaluated with the PRASHNA-PATRA question-answering benchmark.
